Adaptive Finite Element Methods

Phenomena in science and engineering are modeled by systems of partial differential equations having multiscale behavior such as reaction fronts, boundary layers, and molecular interactions that form, evolve, and disappear with time. Standard numerical methods either fail or are inefficient when applied to these problems. Adaptive methods automatically refine, coarsen, and relocate meshes and/or vary method orders in both time and space; hence, they are capable of resolving local nonuniform behavior and provide superior performance to nonadaptive techniques. Adaptive finite element software for both one- and two-dimensional problems have been developed here in the Computer Science Department. Three-dimensional problems often require the use of parallel computers which is discussed here .
